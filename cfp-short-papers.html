Call for Short Papers

We invite work-in-progress, position papers, and preliminary results on the responsible use of AI in educational contexts.

← Back to Call for Papers

Overview

Short papers are ideal for presenting early-stage research, provocative position statements, system demonstrations, or preliminary findings that would benefit from community feedback. They offer an opportunity to share ideas and spark discussion in a less formal setting than full papers.

Topics of Interest

The same topics as full papers apply. We particularly encourage:

  • Ongoing research projects seeking early feedback
  • Position papers on emerging challenges in responsible AI for education
  • Preliminary evaluations of AI-based educational tools
  • Novel research directions and open problems
  • Lessons learned from real-world deployments

Submission Format

Papers must be written in English and formatted according to the Springer LNCS style. Short papers are limited to 6 pages excluding references. Submissions must be anonymized for double-blind review.

Please submit via CMT (link TBA).
