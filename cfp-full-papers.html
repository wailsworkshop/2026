Call for Full Papers

We invite original, unpublished research contributions presenting novel findings, methods, or theoretical frameworks on the responsible use of AI in education.

← Back to Call for Papers

Overview

Full papers should present completed research with substantial empirical or theoretical contributions. Submissions must be original work not currently under review elsewhere.

Topics of Interest

We welcome contributions on (but not limited to) the following topics:

  • Responsible and ethical AI in educational systems
  • Explainability and transparency of AI-based learning tools
  • Fairness, bias detection and mitigation in adaptive learning
  • Human-centered AI design for education
  • AI governance and policy frameworks for education
  • Intelligent tutoring systems and conversational agents
  • Learning analytics and educational data mining
  • AI for accessibility and inclusive education
  • Serious games and game-based learning with AI
  • Large language models in educational contexts

Submission Format

Papers must be written in English and formatted according to the Springer LNCS style. Full papers are limited to 12 pages excluding references. Submissions must be anonymized for double-blind review.
